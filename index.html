About Us

At Nalo Designs, we are passionate about creating sustainable and regenerative landscapes. We believe that permaculture offers a practical and ethical framework for designing systems that meet human needs while enhancing the health of our planet.

Nalo stands for "Nature Always Lives On," reflecting our core belief in the resilience and enduring power of nature.

We are also building the Nalo Network, a collection of "living libraries" - diverse yet interconnected places and communities dedicated to regenerative living.

Our team combines expertise in permaculture principles, ecological design, and sustainable living to provide you with tailored solutions for your home, farm, or community.

Our Services & Costs

We offer a range of services to meet your permaculture design needs. Our pricing is flexible to accommodate different project scales and client relationships. We are open to discussing reduced costs for projects that align with our long-term vision of creating regenerative landscapes, including those that involve a deeper, ongoing relationship and/or partial ownership models.

Our Permaculture Principles

Observe & Interact

Careful observation of natural patterns informs our designs.

Catch & Store Energy

We design systems to capture and store resources like water and sunlight.

Obtain a Yield

Ensuring that our systems produce useful resources.

Apply Self-Regulation & Accept Feedback

Using feedback to adjust our designs and promote self-regulating systems.

Use & Value Renewable Resources

Prioritizing the use of renewable resources to create sustainable systems.

Produce No Waste

Minimizing waste and making the most of all resources.

Design From Patterns to Details

Observing patterns in nature to inform design at all scales.

Integrate Rather Than Segregate

Creating interconnected systems that work together.

Use Small & Slow Solutions

Prioritizing small-scale, manageable solutions for long-term sustainability.

Use & Value Diversity

Recognizing the importance of diversity in creating resilient systems.

Use Edges & Value the Marginal

Utilizing edge effects and valuing often overlooked areas.

Creatively Use & Respond to Change

Adapting to change and using it to our advantage.
